Text 1 (Narrative – A story about a village festival)

Every year, the village holds a big festival. The villagers conclude the event with a fireworks show. They guarantee that no one will miss it. One family always tries to dominate the music competition, but others have a chance. Before the festival, the mayor will confirm the date in a speech. He will greet everyone at the gate. Children entertain the crowd with dances. Some men defend against rain by putting up tents. The rules forbid to anyone from bringing loudspeakers after midnight. The local radio will broadcast the best parts. A young girl will sacrifice her dessert prize to her little brother. The judges will punish for any cheating with a warning. I often glance at the schedule to see what is next. The village wants to retain its old traditions. They calculate the cost of fireworks months in advance. During the final dance, my heart feels like it is sinking with joy.

Anna: The rules forbid to players from arguing with the referee. Ben: Yes, or they’ll get a yellow card. I hope the TV broadcast shows the best goals. Anna: I would sacrifice my snack to see a goal now. Ben: The referee will punish for diving with a free kick for the other team. Anna: I glance at the clock every minute. Ben: We need to retain our position in the league. Anna: The manager will calculate the points after the game. Ben: If we lose, our hopes will be sinking fast.
