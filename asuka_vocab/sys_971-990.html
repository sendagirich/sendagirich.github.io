Text 1: Garden of Justice

In a quiet district of the city, an old gardener named Leo was considered a genius with plants. He believed every seed had merit, no matter how small. One day, strange symptoms appeared on his flowers: yellow spots on every layer of leaf. He had no clue what caused it. “Under any circumstances, don’t use chemicals,” his companion warned. Leo ignored the advice, but the procedure he tried failed. He needed funds for new soil, but had none. Then, a local executive offered a luxury grant. The theme of Leo’s garden became “healing through nature,” and he grew a plant that produced pure oxygen. The boundary between life and death for his garden had been crossed—he saved it, feeling like heaven on earth.

Text 2: The Museum Visit

Anna: Wow, this painting is a genius work! Look at the seeds of color in it. Ben: I see. But the symptoms of old paint worry me—it’s cracking on this layer. Anna: Any clue why? Ben: Not really. Under any circumstances, don’t touch it. This district is famous for art, but the museum has no funds for restoration. Anna: My companion, the director, is an executive. Maybe he can help. Ben: Does he believe in justice for old art? Anna: Yes. The procedure is simple: apply for a grant. It’s a luxury to save such beauty. Ben: The theme here is nature. Look at the sky—it’s like heaven. Anna: And the trees give oxygen. But what’s the boundary between art and life? Ben: That’s a deep question.

Text 3: Space Program: A Report

A genius engineer designed a new rocket. Its seeds of innovation were small but powerful. However, the crew reported symptoms of dizziness—a clue that air quality was poor. Under any circumstances, the launch was delayed. The district where the rocket was built is a high-tech zone. An executive from the space agency approved funds for a new oxygen system. The procedure for installation took one week. It was not a luxury, but a necessity. The theme of the mission was “exploration without harm.” The boundary between Earth and heaven was the goal. Justice meant giving every crew member a safe companion—a robot helper. Each layer of the rocket was checked for leaks.
