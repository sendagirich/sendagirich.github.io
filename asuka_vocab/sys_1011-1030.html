Text 1: Story

A young woman named Clara had a passion for old architecture. She lived in the suburbs but often visited the city. One day, she heard a legend about a hidden treasure from an ancient Empire. She found a dozen old maps in a shop. Her instinct told her to follow them. After a long voyage across the sea, she reached a ruined city. The inhabitants of a nearby village warned her about a curse. She ignored their warning and had a bad cough. She thought her fate was to get sick. But she realized the motive of the curse was to protect the treasure. She used logic to find the hidden room. Inside, there was an old scheme of the Empire written on a wall. She found a box with strange ingredients for a medicine. She used them to test the hypothesis that the curse was just a poison. The medicine cured her cough. She later found out the treasure was a harvest of gold. But she used fossil fuels to burn it and stop the cancer of greed in the empire. An old man called her an insult for burning gold, but she smiled.

Mark: That’s an insult to my intelligence! I’m fine. Anna: The inhabitants of this town say you need rest. They don’t use fossil fuels here, so the air is clean. Mark: My motive for staying is my work. I have a passion for history. Anna: I know. You love old architecture. But this town is in the suburbs. It’s boring. Mark: No! There’s a legend about an old Empire here. I found a dozen old coins. My instinct says there’s more. Anna: Let’s use logic. We can test the hypothesis that the coins are from a treasure. We need ingredients for a map. Mark: That voyage of discovery is my dream. This could be a great harvest. Anna: But your cough could be cancer! Stop this fantasy.
