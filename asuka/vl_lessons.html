Shot descriptions:

  1. The von Trapp children are standing close together on the left side of the room as they sing for their father, the Barroness and Max who stand apart on the right side of the room. The Captain is the biggest person in the shot; he stands straight and looks surprised. He is starting to smile. Max stands behind the Captain looking at the children. He is concentrating and looks impressed. The Barroness sits behind the captain, near Max. She is looking at the Captain’s face. She looks surprised at his reaction to his children.

  1. Captain von Trapp is smiling and hugging his children. The Barroness is sitting behind the von Trapps and looks at them. Max stands near her, leaning against a wall. The barroness looks a little suprised at the Captain’s change of character. Maria is standing outside of the room looking in at the von Traps. She is smiling because the Captain has finally reconnected with his children and is showing them warmth again. She is in the foreground of the shot and so she is bigger than the other characters but she stands alone in the cold looking shadows which contrast with the warmth of the room she looks into.
  • What does this tell us? We know that the Barroness wants to marry Captain von Trapp. As he moves from his possition of standing apart with the adults to hugging his children closely we sense that the relationship between the von Trapp family has changed and that by moving closer to them he is moving farther away from the Barroness.

Character development
  • Characters don’t stay the same all the way through a film; they change as the story develops.
  • Look at an early scene when Maria meets Captain von Trapp and describe the Captain.
  • Now look at the Edelweiss scene. Describe the Captain in this scene and describe how he has changed.
  • Relationships between characters also change.
  • How has Maria’s relationship with Captain von Trapp Changed?
  • How has the children’s relationship with Maria Changed?
  • Changes in characters personality and relationships are often written in the script ans said directly.
  • But, we are often not told directly about changes. How do we learn about these changes?
Text - context - subtext review
Text
  • The story written in the script and said and acted by the actors on screen.
  • What are the characters saying?
  • What are the characters doing?
Context
  • The setting: when and where the story takes place.
  • The situations and relationships that events take place in.
  • This often includes historical events that are not in the film.
  • For films that are old and from different cultures and different languages, we need to work harder to understand the context.
  • Where are the characters?
  • What is happening around them?
Subtext
  • Reading between the lines.
  • Understanding meanings that are not said.
  • What is really going on?
