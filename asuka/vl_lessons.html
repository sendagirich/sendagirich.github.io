4. Introduce shots
  • Match the words to the definitions.
     A. Shot
 B. Scene
 C. Frame
    1. A continuous action or event in a single place and time, made of one or more shots, that tells a part of the story.
    2. A single, continuous take from the camera, from the moment it starts recording to when it stops, representing a specific viewpoint or angle.
    3. A single still image that is part of in a sequence that show movement in film when they are played together.
Shots
5. Prepare your film idea.
  1. Decide on the idea for your film.
  2. Check key areas:
    • What is the genre?
    • Where is it set?
    • When is it set?
    • Who are the characters?
    • What is the plot?
  3. Prepare your script
    • Look at the HSM script below.
    • Write your script like this. Write the scene with CUT TO. Then write your dialogue or narration.
HSM script (scene one)
  • CUT TO: EXT. SKI LODGE, NEW YEAR’S EVE
  • High School Musical logo appears on screen.
  • CUT TO: INT. SKI LODGE, NEW YEAR’S EVE
  • The ski lodge is alive with excitement as the inhabitants enjoy the festivities. Gabriella Montez sits on the sofa reading as Miss Montez approaches.
  • MISS MONTEZ: Gabby, it’s New Year’s Eve. Enough reading.
  • GABRIELLA: But, Mom, I’m almost done
  • MISS MONTEZ: The teen party? I’ve laid out your best clothes, Come get ready.
  • GABRIELLA: Can I have my book back?
  • Miss Montez hands the book back to Gabriella.
  • GABRIELLA: Thank you.
  • MISS MONTEZ: Come on.
