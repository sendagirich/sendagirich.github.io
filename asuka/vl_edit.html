Editing

  • Film makers put the film together with the editor.
  • They choose how to arrange the shots they have taken.
  • They arrange the audio.
  • They add music and special effects.
  • Editing is now one on computer but was originally done by hand and so uses language from when editing was done by hand.

The transitions

  • cut
    • An instant switch or transition from one shot to another / scene
  • fade
    • A shot dissolves to or from a solid colour
    • Fade to black often ends a film / can signal the end of a chapter.
    • Fade to white - often used to signify shifting to a dream.
  • dissolve
    • Dissolve gradually changes to another shot.
    • Can suggest a longer passage of time, memory or a dream.
  • match cut
    • Any transition that matches elements from the previous scene to match the next.
  • iris
    • Like a fade but ends or begins with a small circle.
  • wipe
    • Shot A is replaced by shot B from a specific direction
    • Can give a retro feel.
  • passing transition
    • Uses a moving character or object to change to the next scene or a moving camera to move to the next scene.
  • whip pan
    • A quick pan used to change scene.
  • smash cut
    • A sudden cut that suddenly contrasts visual images or sound to shock the viewer.
  • J-cut and L-cuts
    • A change of shot or scene where sound and visuals overlap.
