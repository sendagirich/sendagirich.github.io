Figure 1: High School Musical 1

An over the shoulder shot. The subject’s pale blue sweater contrasts with the bright red of the locker room and his team mates’ clothes that dominate the rest of the frame.

Figure 2: The Sound of Music 1

Two shot. The two subjects are in shadow but backlighting highlights each figure against the dark background.

Figure 3: The Sound of Music 2

A one shot from a low angle looking up at the subject. She is illuminated from below with a strong artificial light that casts strong shadows against the wall. The strong shadows make the scene more dramatic. She is in the center of the shot but a pillar and shadow are in the right third that balances the composition. The pale browns and blues of her clothes blend with the colours of the wallpaper and stairs in the rest of the frame.

Figure 4: The Sound of Music 3

An over the shoulder shot. The subject is in the left third of the frame. Warm light from the house windows illuminate the subject. Her red dress, pink skin and blonde hair all reflect this warmth. A cold moonlight highlights both figures and separates the cool blue of the Captain, who is facing away from the camera, from the cold shadows of the wall of the house in the background. The warmth of the Baroness makes her look vulnerable in contrast to the cold blues of her surroundings in the moment that she is being told that the captain loves Maria and not the Baroness.
