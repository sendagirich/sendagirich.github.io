Very Formal Rejection

Subject: Regarding Your Submission to Asuka English Journal

Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name],

We are writing to you regarding the submission of your essay titled “[Essay Title]” to the Asuka English Journal. After thorough consideration and a rigorous review process, we regret to inform you that we will not be proceeding with the publication of your work in our journal.

We would like to acknowledge the evident effort and scholarly dedication that went into your essay. However, our editorial team has concluded that the piece does not align perfectly with the thematic focus and the current editorial needs of our journal. We believe that the essay could benefit from an expanded analysis of the primary arguments and a closer examination of the contemporary sources that support your thesis.

We encourage you to continue refining your work, perhaps by further elaborating on your thesis and considering the inclusion of a more diverse range of scholarly viewpoints. Rest assured, the scholarly community is expansive, and many platforms may find your work to be a perfect fit for their publications.

We appreciate your interest in the Asuka English Journal and sincerely hope that you will consider us again for future submissions. We wish you the best in your continuous scholarly endeavors.

Yours sincerely,

Richard Williams (B.A., M.A. Art History) Editor Asuka English Journal

Very Formal Rejection
  • Choice of Language: The language is highly professional, using technical terms and sophisticated phrases (“rigorous review process,” “expanded analysis”).
  • Salutation: Uses a formal salutation with the recipient’s title and last name.
  • Sentence Structure: Complex and carefully constructed sentences are evident throughout.
  • Sign-Off: Includes a formal sign-off with the sender’s full name, position, and the institution’s name.
Formal Rejection

Subject: Submission Feedback for “[Essay Title]”

Dear [First Name],

Thank you for submitting your essay, “[Essay Title],” for consideration in Asuka English Journal. After a detailed review process, we regret to inform you that we are unable to include your essay in our publication at this time.

Your piece presents compelling arguments and is written with a clear passion for the subject. However, it does not fully align with the thematic direction we have set for our next issue. Moreover, we suggest deepening your research to provide a more robust analysis and considering a slightly more engaging narrative style to enhance readability for our diverse audience.

Your work has clear merit, and we believe with some revisions, it could find a suitable platform for publication. We encourage you to pursue this, and please do consider us for future submissions.

We wish you all the best with your writing endeavors and hope to see your work submitted to us again.

Warm regards,

Richard Williams Editor Asuka English Journal

Formal Rejection
  • Choice of Language: While professional, the language is slightly less technical than the very formal example, aiming to be clear and direct but still polite.
  • Salutation: A more personal tone by using the recipient’s first name, yet still maintaining a level of formality.
  • Sentence Structure: Sentences are structured formally but are more straightforward and less complex.
  • Sign-Off: Ends with “Warm regards,” indicating a formal but friendly tone, along with the sender’s full name and position.
Semi-formal Rejection

Subject: Your Essay Submission: [Essay Title]

Hi [First Name],

Thanks so much for sending your essay, “[Essay Title],” our way. We’ve had a chance to review it, and we wanted to get back to you.

Unfortunately, we can’t fit your essay into our publication at this point. Your thesis is intriguing, and you’ve certainly done your homework on the subject. However, we feel that the essay might benefit from a bit more personality and a tighter focus on your main argument to really stand out.

We’re always on the lookout for fresh perspectives, so this definitely shouldn’t deter you from submitting again in the future. Maybe a slight tweak here and there, and who knows? It might just be the perfect fit for our next issue.

Thanks again for thinking of us, and all the best with your writing!

Cheers,

Richard Editor [Publication Name]

Semi-formal Rejection
  • Choice of Language: The language begins to adopt a conversational tone but retains a sense of professionalism (“Your thesis is intriguing,” “fresh perspectives”).
  • Salutation: Uses the recipient’s first name, creating a more personal connection.
  • Sentence Structure: The structure loosens slightly, incorporating elements of casual speech within a professional framework.
  • Sign-Off: The closure is friendly and encourages future submissions, signed off with the sender’s first name and role.
Informal Rejection

Subject: Quick Note about Your Submission

Hey [First Name],

Just wanted to drop you a quick note about your essay, “[Essay Title].” We gave it a read, and there’s some really cool stuff in there. Unfortunately, it’s not quite what we’re looking for right now.

Your writing’s got spark, but the essay could use a tighter focus. Try honing in on your main idea a bit more. And hey, a little more of your own voice in there wouldn’t hurt—it’s what makes your work unique, after all.

Don’t let this stop you from sending stuff our way in the future. Keep at it, and I’m sure we’ll find the right fit sooner rather than later.

Best,

Richard Williams

Informal Rejection
  • Choice of Language: Noticeably more conversational and direct, using everyday language and expressions (“got some really cool stuff,” “tighter focus”).
  • Salutation: A very casual approach, using only the recipient’s first name.
  • Sentence Structure: Sentences are more relaxed, mimicking natural speech patterns more closely.
  • Sign-Off: A casual sign-off with just the sender’s first name and the publication name, embodying a peer-to-peer exchange.
Very Informal Rejection

Subject: Re: “[Essay Title]” Submission

Hey [First Name],

Thanks a ton for letting us check out your work! We’ve had a look at “[Essay Title],” and here’s the scoop: it’s not a match for us right now, but dang, you’ve got some good thoughts rolling.

Your style’s pretty neat, but the piece could do with a bit more digging into the nitty-gritty of your main point. And throw in some more of YOU in there. Your voice, your takes—let’s see what you’ve really got.

Don’t take this as a full stop, yeah? Just a lil bump in the road. Keep sending your stuff out there, and don’t forget about us for future submissions.

Keep rocking it,

Rich [Your Nickname/Role if any] [Mag/Blog Name]

Very Informal Rejection
  • Choice of Language: Extremely casual, almost as if speaking to a friend, using colloquialisms and relaxed phrases (“dang, you’ve got some good thoughts rolling”).
  • Salutation: Extremely casual and direct, using the recipient’s first name with no formalities.
  • Sentence Structure: Embraces a loose, conversational structure that breaks traditional formalities (“Just a lil bump in the road”).
  • Sign-Off: Very informal, possibly including a nickname or role in a playful manner, and emphasizing the casual nature of the relationship.
