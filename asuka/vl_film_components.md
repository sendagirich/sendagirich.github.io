

# Parts of a film with definitions

<table border="2" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="6" rules="groups" frame="hsides">

<tr>
<td class="org-left">&#xa0;</td>
<td class="org-left">&#xa0;</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Title</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The name of the film.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Genre</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The type of film or TV program, such as drama, comedy, horror, etc.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Director</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The person in control of making the film who gives instructions to the crew and actors.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Cast</b></td>
<td class="org-left">Actors and actresses who appear in the film or TV program.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Plot</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The main story line of the film.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Shot</b></td>
<td class="org-left">A continuous camera recording that makes up a scene.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Setting</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The time and place where the film&rsquo;s or show&rsquo;s story occurs.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Cinematography</b></td>
<td class="org-left">Creating the  visual images of the film.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Soundtrack</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The music that accompanies a film or TV program, often made for the film.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Special Effects</b></td>
<td class="org-left">Visual or practical effects created to enhance the realism or artistic value of scenes.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Script/screenplay</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The written text of a film or TV program, including dialogue and directions for acting and camera work.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Dialogue</b></td>
<td class="org-left">Spoken conversation between characters in a film or TV program.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Character Development</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The way characters undergo change or growth through the story.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Scene</b></td>
<td class="org-left">A unit of the story that usually takes place in a single setting, such as a room or building.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Opening Scene</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The first scene or sequence in a film or TV program, setting the tone and premise.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Closing Scene</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The final scene or sequence in a film or TV program, often resolving the plot or leaving a lasting impression.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Costumes</b></td>
<td class="org-left">The clothing worn by characters in a film or TV program, which can define time period, culture, or character traits.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Editing</b></td>
<td class="org-left">Using a computer to assemble shots to make a film and adding sound and special effects.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td class="org-left"><b>Background Score</b></td>
<td class="org-left">Instrumental music that plays in the background of scenes, often used to set the mood or emphasize emotions.</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
