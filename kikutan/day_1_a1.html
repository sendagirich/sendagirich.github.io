My New Friend

Last week, I made a new friend. His name is Leo. Leo likes to create stories. “Create” means to make something. He helps me with my English. This helps me develop my speaking. Leo likes to lead when we play games.

When we read books, I pay attention. I follow the words with my finger. Leo provides help if I don’t know a word. He suggests reading slowly. Our books include new words. Leo allows me to ask questions.

Sometimes, I fill the pages with notes. Leo explains the hard words. This helps me improve my English.

I support Leo in math. I describe how to solve problems. Leo says, “You are a good friend.”

Having a friend like Leo is fun. We learn and help each other.

Planting My First Garden

I decided to create my own garden. “Create” means to make something new. My mom helps me. She shows me how to plant seeds. This helps me develop a new skill. My job is to lead in watering the plants.

Every day, I pay attention to the plants. I follow what my mom says. She provides the seeds and water. She suggests planting flowers and vegetables. Our garden will include tomatoes and roses. My mom allows me to choose some seeds.

I fill the pots with soil. My mom explains how to plant the seeds. Watching the plants grow helps me improve in gardening.

My dad supports our project. We describe our garden plans. I say, “I want to grow big tomatoes.” Mom says, “The roses will be beautiful.”

Starting a garden is exciting. It teaches me to care for plants.

My First Bicycle Ride

I remember when I decided to learn how to ride a bicycle. My goal was to create my own adventures. “Create” means to make something that wasn’t there before. My dad helps me start. He knows that riding a bike can develop my confidence. He carefully leads the way, showing me where to go.

At first, I pay attention to every move. I follow the path my dad chooses. He provides the helmet and knee pads for safety. He suggests I start on the grass to fall softly. Our journey includes both falls and successes. My dad allows me to go at my own pace.

As I fill my afternoons with practice, my dad explains how to balance and pedal. These efforts help me improve quickly.

With my family’s support, I learn to ride smoothly. They describe how proud they are when they see me glide by. I say, “Look at me go!” My dad says, “You’re doing great!”

Learning to ride a bike opens up a world of fun. It’s an experience that teaches me to keep trying, no matter how many times I might fall.

Making My First Cake

Yesterday, I wanted to create a cake for my mom’s birthday. “Create” means to make something special. My grandma helps me. She knows how to make many cakes and can develop my baking skills. She gently leads me through the recipe.

We start by paying attention to the ingredients. I follow the steps that grandma provides. She suggests we make a chocolate cake because it’s mom’s favorite. Our recipe includes eggs, flour, sugar, and cocoa. Grandma allows me to measure and pour the ingredients.

I fill the mixing bowl while grandma explains how to mix without making a mess. This way, we both help to improve the cake batter.

With grandma’s support, I feel more confident. We describe what we’re doing as we add each ingredient. I say, “This smells good!” Grandma says, “You’re doing a great job!”

Making a cake is exciting and a little bit challenging. But with grandma’s help, I learn a lot about baking.

Building a Birdhouse

Last weekend, I decided to create something special for our garden. “Create” means to make something new. My uncle helps me with the project. He knows that building a birdhouse can develop my woodworking skills. He leads by showing me the design we will follow.

First, we pay close attention to the safety rules. I follow my uncle’s instructions carefully. He provides all the tools we need. He suggests starting with a simple house. Our birdhouse will include a small entrance for birds and a platform. My uncle allows me to decide where we should hang it in the garden.

I fill the birdhouse with seeds to welcome the birds. My uncle explains how to smooth the wood and assemble the pieces. These steps help me improve my building skills.

My family supports our project. We describe the birdhouse with pride. I say, “It’s perfect for our garden!” My uncle says, “You did a great job!”

Building the birdhouse is not only fun but also teaches me patience and precision.
