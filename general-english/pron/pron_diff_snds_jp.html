Difficult sounds for Japanese speakers

Look at the Pronunciation key terms words here if you need to review them.

Japanese has only five main vowel sounds that are similar to (but not exactly the same as) vowel sounds in English:

/​æ​/ (あ) (cat)
/​I​/ (い) (in)
/​ʊ​/ (う) (put)
/​e​/ (え) (bed)
/​ɒ​/ (お) (hot)

English has more sounds than Japanese. Japanese does not have these sounds:

Pracising difficult sounds

  • Look at the following videos and practise saying the words.
  • Use a mirror (or your phone camera) to look at your mouth when you practice these sounds.
  • Remember you do not have to speak like a native speaker or say everything perfectly so don’t worry about making mistakes. Also remember that understanding English pronunciation will improve your listing ability.
  • Try to listen for these sounds next time you hear spoken English from your teacher or in a movie.

Try these tongue twisters

Sea shells

  • She sells seashells by the seashore,
  • The shells she sells are seashells, I’m sure.
  • So if she sells seashells on the seashore,
  • Then I’m sure she sells seashore shells.

Red lorry (lorry = truck in British English)

  • Red lorry, yellow lorry, red lorry, yellow lorry.

Lucky rabbits

  • Lucky rabbits leap rapidly over lazy lions.

Consonant clusters

  • Remember not to add a vowel sound between consonants if they should not bee there.

    fly not fulai fry not furai play not pulay bring not buringu
