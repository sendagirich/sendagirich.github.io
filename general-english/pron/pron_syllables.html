Introduction to syllables

  • A syllable is a unit of sound in a word. It is the beat of a word if we clap out its rhythm.
  • The number of syllables and the number of letters are not the same.

  • All the following words have one syllable:

    Word Syllables Letters
    I 1 1
    strengths 1 9
    it 1 2
    scrunched 1 9

Consonant blends

  • Because katakana is always consonant-vowel „Ç´, „Ç≠, „ÇØ, „Ç±, „Ç≥ you will add extra vowels (and therefore syllables) when consonants come together (consonants blends) such as pl, st,
  • Consonant blends can be at the begging, middle or end of words.

(2-letter):

  • L-blends: bl-, cl-, fl-, gl-, pl-, sl- (e.g., black, clip, fly)
  • R-blends: br-, cr-, dr-, fr-, gr-, pr-, tr- (e.g., brick, drop, green)
  • S-blends: sc-, sk-, sm-, sn-, sp-, st-, sw- (e.g., sky, smile, stop)
  • Other: tw-, dw- (e.g., twin, dwarf)

(3-letter):

  • spr-, str-, scr-, spl-, squ- (e.g., spring, street, splash)

    Final Consonant Blends

  • -nt, -nd, -nk, -ng (e.g., tent, hand, sink, long)
  • -st, -sk, -sp, -ft, -lt, -lp, -lk (e.g., fast, ask, help)
  • -mp, -pt, -ct (e.g., jump, kept, act)
